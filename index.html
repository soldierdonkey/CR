Extra Info:

The equation of Cellular Respiration is: Glucose + 6O2 = 6CO2 + 6H2O + LOTS OF ATP (up to 38)

In Cellular Respiration, glucose is broken down to create energy in the form of ATP. This lengthy process must occur, because glucose is'nt like a car battery that you plug into your meatgrinder, it's more like a puddle of deisel, that needs a generator to be used.

When looking at the two equations, Photosynthesis, and Cellular Respiration, they look very similar, except reversed. Even some of the processes in Cellular Respiration look like Photosynthesis played in reverse.

The structure of the Mitochondria supports the later parts of Cellular Respiration that take place in it by creation a semi-permeable membrane for the ATP synthase, and creating an aerobic environment for the Link reaction and the Krebs Cycle.
